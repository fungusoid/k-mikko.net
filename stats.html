A Practical Guide to Basic Statistics

Understand data with these fundamental statistical concepts.

What is Statistics?

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, presenting, and organizing data. In a world full of data, understanding basic statistics is a crucial skill. It helps us make sense of information, spot trends, and make informed decisions. From analyzing business performance to understanding scientific research, statistics is everywhere.

Measures of Central Tendency & Dispersion

These statistics summarize the "center" and "spread" of your data, giving a quick overview of its main characteristics.

Enter numbers separated by commas.

Mean (Average)

The sum of all values divided by the count of values. It's sensitive to outliers.

Median

The middle value when the data is sorted. If there's an even number of values, it's the average of the two middle ones.

Mode

The value that appears most frequently. A dataset can have one mode, more than one, or no mode.

Standard Deviation

Measures how spread out the numbers are from the mean. A low value means data is clustered around the mean.

Linear Regression

Linear regression is a way to model the relationship between two variables. It finds the straight line that best "fits" the data points on a scatter plot. This line can be used to make predictions. For example, you could use regression to predict a student's exam score based on the number of hours they studied.
